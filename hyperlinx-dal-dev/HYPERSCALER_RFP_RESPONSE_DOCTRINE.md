# Hyperscaler RFP Response Doctrine

The reusable commercial platform is **Teralinx Bid Engine**. Google Helium is the first production fixture and precedent, not a customer-specific execution path.

Hyperscaler RFPs become reusable opportunity structures:

- Customer
- Opportunity
- Requested routes
- Customer sites
- Network intent
- Required attachments
- Response contacts
- Submission deadlines
- Vendor response mappings

Each requested span should produce:

- Design Launch Session
- RouteCandidate
- CenterlineRoute
- StationedCorridor
- CorridorTakeoff
- ProposedGraph
- PreliminaryQuotePackage
- CivilMixEstimate
- VendorResponsePreview

All outputs are budgetary and pending engineering verification.

Core platform logic must remain customer-neutral. Customer fixtures may carry workbook mappings, route requirements, and commercial assumptions, but planning, visualization, proposal, and export orchestration remain reusable across hyperscaler and carrier opportunities.

# Corridor Design Standards Doctrine

Corridor Design Standards define what corridor objects require before they may support engineered decisions.

This doctrine is advisory to Prism and mandatory context for Route Engineering review. It does not create routing, scoring, recommendations, persistence, ScopeVersions, or authority by itself.

## Core Rule

Sales does not determine engineering standards.

Prism may advise.

Corridor lenses may prioritize.

Design Standards define what each object requires.

Route Engineering reviews, redlines, approves, or rejects.

Human engineering approval remains authoritative.

## Authority Boundary

Corridor objects are evidence and planning context.

Design Standards describe the engineering questions that must be answered for those objects.

Route Engineering owns the approval of design feasibility, object placement, optical assumptions, route diversity, capacity claims, and exceptions.

## How Standards Fit The Corridor Chain

Translate creates normalized evidence.

Corridor objects classify the evidence.

Corridor lenses decide what matters for a commercial or strategic view.

Reference Architecture defines what must be considered for the customer ask.

Design Standards define what those objects require.

Prism scoring may evaluate only after standards context exists.

Recommendation remains advisory.

Route Engineering remains authoritative.

## Sales Boundary

Sales may define:

- customer
- endpoints
- desired service
- commercial target
- preferred lens
- product intent

Sales may not define:

- regen spacing
- ADM placement
- optical reach
- restoration design
- route diversity sufficiency
- engineering feasibility
- final capacity availability
- permitting assumptions as truth

## Route Engineering Authority

Route Engineering owns:

- design validation
- redlines
- route certification
- object approval
- standards exceptions
- ScopeVersion handoff

## Standards Exceptions

A standards exception is a review object, not an approval.

An exception may be requested when commercial or strategic intent conflicts with standard guidance, missing evidence, or preliminary engineering constraints.

Exception status values:

- REQUESTED
- APPROVED
- REJECTED
- SUPERSEDED

No approval workflow is implemented in this phase.

## Constitutional Rule

Lens determines which objects matter.

Reference Architecture determines what must be considered.

Design Standards determine what those objects require.

Route Engineering determines whether the design may become executable truth.

## Prism Recommendation Relationship

Prism Recommendation may use design standards to identify review blockers, required evidence, and required Route Engineering reviews.

Unresolved standards may make a recommendation conditional or blocked.

Recommendation remains advisory.

Human review is required.

Route Engineering remains authority.
